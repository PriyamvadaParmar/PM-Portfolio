## Context
WhatsApp is widely used not only for personal conversations, but also for **semi-official communication** such as school updates, housing society notices, local community coordination, and informal work discussions.

While the product excels at simplicity and speed, these newer usage patterns introduce challenges that were not part of its original design intent.

---

## Target User
**Primary User:**  
Individuals who are part of multiple WhatsApp groups where messages have **real-world consequences**, such as:
- Parents in school groups
- Residents in housing society groups
- Members of local or community groups
- Professionals using WhatsApp informally for coordination

These users are not looking for a work tool, but they **depend on WhatsApp for important information**.

---

## User Problem
Users struggle to **identify, track, and respond to important messages** in WhatsApp groups because:

- All messages are presented with equal visual priority  
- High-volume conversations quickly bury critical information  
- There is no lightweight way to distinguish urgent or official messages from casual chatter  

As a result, users frequently miss or overlook messages that require action.
